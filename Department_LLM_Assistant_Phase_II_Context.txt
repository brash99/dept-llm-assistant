Department LLM Assistant — Phase II Context

We are continuing development of the Department LLM Assistant project.

Project Vision

The goal is not to build a chatbot.

The goal is to build an institutional semantic layer (or “semantic
ecosystem”) that supports explainable, evidence-based decision support
for university administration.

The long-term vision is a semantic “digital twin” of the institution.

Core principle: > The AI should assist human decision makers, not
replace them.

Every conclusion should be traceable back to evidence.

Decision Support Vision

Move from Question Answering to Decision Briefs.

Representative institutional questions: - What additional resources
would be required to launch a Mechanical Engineering major? - What are
the implications of replacing (or not replacing) a retiring faculty
member? - What departments are most affected under a particular
enrollment scenario?

The system should retrieve and synthesize evidence regarding: - Faculty
expertise - Curriculum - Facilities - Laboratory equipment -
Accreditation - Budget - Strategic planning - Historical documents

The AI should produce an explainable decision brief supported by
citations.

Design Philosophy

The most intellectually demanding parts of the system are: - Corpus
quality - Semantic organization - Retrieval quality - Explainability -
Benchmark design - Evidence synthesis

The LLM performs the final reasoning step rather than providing the
primary intelligence of the system.

Working Style

Edward is a physicist and computer scientist who prefers: - Quantitative
evaluation - Benchmarking - Explainability - Careful architectural
thinking - Understanding why something works rather than simply that it
works

The project is treated as both an engineering effort and a research
program.
