# Institutional Semantic Observatory (ISO)

## Decision Briefs

**Version 0.1**

---

# Introduction

The primary product of the Institutional Semantic Observatory is the Decision Brief.

A Decision Brief is not an AI-generated opinion.

It is an evidence-backed synthesis of Institutional Memory constructed to support human decision making.

Decision Briefs therefore emphasize explanation rather than recommendation.

---

# Purpose

Decision Briefs answer institutional questions by:

- assembling relevant evidence
- identifying areas of agreement
- exposing uncertainty
- highlighting missing information
- documenting provenance
- preserving explainability

The objective is not to automate decisions.

The objective is to improve institutional understanding.

---

# Decision Support Rather Than Decision Making

ISO intentionally separates evidence from judgment.

The observatory may conclude:

"The available evidence suggests..."

It never concludes:

"The institution should..."

Final decisions remain the responsibility of institutional leaders.

---

# Structure

Every Decision Brief follows a consistent structure.

Typical sections include:

- Executive Summary
- Institutional Question
- Evidence Summary
- Supporting Evidence
- Areas of Agreement
- Areas of Uncertainty
- Missing Information
- Strategic Considerations
- Recommended Follow-Up
- Sources

This consistency improves transparency and comparability across institutional questions.

---

# Evidence-Driven Generation

Decision Briefs are constructed from retrieved Institutional Memory.

The language model does not search independently.

Instead, it synthesizes evidence already identified by the observatory.

This preserves provenance and enables complete citation of supporting observations.

---

# Explainability

Every statement in a Decision Brief should be traceable to retrieved evidence.

Readers should be able to determine:

- where evidence originated
- why it was retrieved
- how strongly it supports a conclusion

Explainability is therefore a first-class design goal.

---

# Uncertainty

Institutional evidence is often incomplete.

Rather than concealing uncertainty, Decision Briefs explicitly identify it.

Examples include:

- conflicting observations
- insufficient evidence
- missing historical records
- outdated documentation

Recognizing uncertainty is an important part of responsible decision support.

---

# Missing Information

One distinguishing feature of ISO Decision Briefs is the explicit identification of missing evidence.

For example:

- current enrollment reports
- recent assessment data
- budget projections
- facilities utilization
- staffing analyses

These gaps become recommendations for future observation.

Thus, every Decision Brief contributes to improving Institutional Memory.

---

# Strategic Considerations

Decision Briefs distinguish observations from implications.

Observations describe the institution.

Strategic considerations discuss how those observations may influence planning.

This distinction preserves the separation between evidence and judgment.

---

# Recommended Follow-Up

Decision Briefs conclude with recommended avenues for additional observation.

These recommendations may include:

- acquiring new evidence
- consulting institutional experts
- collecting additional measurements
- validating assumptions
- updating historical records

Decision support therefore becomes an iterative observational process.

---

# Relationship to the Observatory

Decision Briefs represent one possible interpretation of the current semantic ecosystem.

As Institutional Memory evolves, future Decision Briefs may differ because the institution itself has changed.

Decision Briefs therefore capture both:

- the institutional question

and

- the current observational state of Institutional Memory.

---

# Beyond Reports

Decision Briefs are not static reports.

They are dynamic scientific summaries generated from continuously evolving institutional observations.

As the observatory grows, Decision Briefs become progressively richer, more complete, and more representative of institutional reality.

---

# Closing Statement

Decision Briefs embody the central philosophy of ISO:

**Human judgment is strongest when supported by transparent, explainable, evidence-based institutional understanding.**

Their purpose is not to replace institutional leaders.

Their purpose is to help them see the institution more clearly.
