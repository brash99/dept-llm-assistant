# From Question Answering to Institutional Decision Support

> Building an explainable semantic layer for evidence-based university decision making.

---

# Introduction

The Institutional Knowledge Framework began as an investigation into Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).

Its original objective was to build a local question-answering system capable of retrieving information from a departmental document collection.

As the project evolved, however, it became clear that answering questions was not the most interesting problem.

The more significant challenge was helping institutional leaders make better decisions.

The framework is therefore evolving beyond question answering toward explainable decision support.

---

# The Limitation of Question Answering

Question-answering systems are designed to retrieve information.

For example,

> *What does the faculty handbook say about sabbatical eligibility?*

or

> *Where is the ABET assessment schedule documented?*

These are valuable capabilities.

However, university administration frequently requires answers to a different class of questions.

For example,

- Should we launch a new academic program?
- Should a retiring faculty member be replaced?
- Which departments become vulnerable under changing enrollment?
- What facilities would be required for a new engineering major?
- Which strategic initiatives are most strongly supported by existing institutional plans?

These questions rarely have a single document containing the answer.

Instead, they require synthesizing evidence drawn from many parts of the institution.

---

# From Documents to Evidence

Institutional decisions are rarely based upon isolated documents.

They emerge from evidence distributed across multiple sources.

Relevant information may include:

- curriculum documents
- faculty expertise
- accreditation reports
- laboratory inventories
- strategic plans
- committee reports
- historical decisions
- budget documents
- enrollment data

The challenge is therefore not document retrieval.

The challenge is evidence synthesis.

---

# Decision Briefs

The primary output of the next phase of the project is no longer an answer.

It is a **Decision Brief**.

A Decision Brief synthesizes evidence relevant to a particular institutional question while preserving complete transparency regarding the sources supporting each conclusion.

Rather than replacing human judgment, the framework provides decision makers with a structured summary of institutional evidence.

---

# Explainability

Institutional decisions require trust.

Consequently, every conclusion generated by the framework should be traceable back to supporting evidence.

The framework therefore emphasizes:

- source citations
- retrieval transparency
- explainable reasoning
- uncertainty identification
- evidence provenance

Decision makers should always understand *why* the system reached a particular conclusion.

---

# Human-Centered AI

The framework is designed to augment—not replace—human expertise.

The objective is not autonomous decision making.

The objective is better informed decision making.

Human administrators remain responsible for:

- interpreting evidence
- balancing competing priorities
- applying institutional values
- making final decisions

The AI serves as an intelligent research assistant capable of rapidly synthesizing institutional knowledge.

---

# Representative Questions

Examples of institutional questions include:

- What additional resources would be required to launch a Mechanical Engineering major?

- What are the likely consequences of replacing—or not replacing—a retiring faculty member?

- Which departments are most affected under a projected enrollment scenario?

- Which existing courses could support a proposed interdisciplinary program?

- Which strategic priorities are consistently reflected across institutional planning documents?

These questions require reasoning across the institutional semantic ecosystem rather than retrieving isolated documents.

---

# Toward an Institutional Semantic Layer

The long-term objective is to construct an institutional semantic layer.

Rather than viewing institutional information as thousands of disconnected files, the framework organizes knowledge into a coherent semantic representation that can support retrieval, synthesis, and reasoning.

This semantic layer becomes the foundation upon which future decision-support capabilities are built.

---

# The Long-Term Vision

The Institutional Knowledge Framework is intended to evolve through three increasingly sophisticated stages.

## Phase I

Construct a trustworthy semantic representation of institutional knowledge.

Deliverable:

An explainable Retrieval-Augmented Generation system.

---

## Phase II

Develop evidence-based decision support through Decision Briefs and institutional reasoning.

Deliverable:

An AI-assisted decision-support platform.

---

## Phase III

Develop an institutional semantic digital twin capable of supporting scenario analysis, strategic planning, and probabilistic forecasting.

Deliverable:

An explainable institutional intelligence system.

---

# Guiding Principle

The primary deliverable is not a chatbot.

The primary deliverable is an explainable institutional semantic layer that enables better human decision making.
