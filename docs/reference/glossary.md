# ISO Glossary

This glossary defines the canonical terminology used throughout the Institutional Semantic Observatory architecture.

---

## Observation

A recorded description of some aspect of an institution at a particular moment in time.

Observations are temporal.

They may later become outdated, but they are never retroactively false.

---

## SourceDocument

A canonical observation acquired from an external source.

Examples include:

- PDFs
- Policies
- Budgets
- Catalogs
- Meeting minutes
- Institutional web pages
- Database exports

---

## Knowledge Object

A normalized representation of an observation suitable for downstream processing.

Knowledge Objects preserve observations and provenance.

They do not contain institutional interpretations.

---

## Institutional Memory

The append-only collection of canonical observations acquired by ISO.

Institutional Memory preserves history rather than current state.

---

## Distributed Institutional Observation

The process of continuously observing multiple governed institutional information sources and transforming them into Institutional Memory.

---

## Semantic Ecosystem

The complete collection of observations together with their semantic relationships.

The ecosystem possesses measurable properties including coverage, diversity, dominance, fragmentation, and temporal depth.

---

## Observatory Assessment

A deterministic assessment of the quality and completeness of retrieved evidence before LLM reasoning begins.

---

## Semantic Control Plane

The reasoning layer that establishes institutional context before retrieval and synthesis.

---

## Decision Brief

An explainable synthesis of retrieved institutional evidence intended to support—not replace—human decision making.

---

## Provenance

Metadata describing where an observation originated, when it was acquired, how it was obtained, and its institutional authority.
