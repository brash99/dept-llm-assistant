# ISO Glossary

## Six-layer architecture

The permanent ISO architecture: Evidence Layer, Semantic Layer, Reasoning Layer, Evidence Fitness, Scenario Modeling, and Institutional Digital Twin. Subsystems and historical phases do not replace these layers.

## Observation

A provenance-bearing record acquired from a governed source at a particular time.

## SourceDocument

The acquisition contract for source content and metadata contributed by an observer. Source documents can originate from files, websites, curated catalogs, or other authorized sources.

## Knowledge Object

A normalized representation of an observation used downstream. Knowledge Objects store facts/content and provenance; they do not store derived institutional judgments.

## Constitutional Knowledge Object

A Knowledge Object derived from an identified, configured institutional source and annotated with declared principles, constitutional type, institutional scope, and validity metadata. It preserves sourced values rather than invented values.

## Institutional Memory

The provenance-preserving collection of observations and Knowledge Objects. Complete append-only temporal history is a design objective; not every current acquisition path yet provides a complete historical institutional record.

## Evidence class

A broad deterministic reasoning category such as Constitutional, Institutional, Planning, Historical, External Standard, External Comparator, or Background.

## Evidence role

A claim-safety label more specific than evidence class, such as Institutional Self-Study, Formal External Standard, Departmental Report, Institutional Operating Record, Planning Document, Strategic / Constitutional Document, or Contextual Reference.

## Institutional self-study

A locally authored accreditation or program narrative. It can establish what the institution or program reports, but it is not the formal accreditor standard and does not by itself establish current compliance.

## Formal external standard

A requirement or criterion issued by an external authority. It can establish that a constraint exists but not that a local unit complies or how much margin it has.

## Document family

A deterministic grouping of drafts, revisions, filename variants, self-study packages, or criterion-response variants representing closely related evidence. Family members do not count as independent corroboration merely because they are separate files.

## Semantic Control Plane

The Semantic Layer subsystem that produces institutional orientation, constitutional orientation, guarded program resolution, and question scope before retrieval. It does not retrieve evidence or recommend an action.

## Question scope

A deterministic classification of whether a question concerns a single entity, multiple entities, the institution as a whole, or remains unresolved.

## Institutional topology

A curated set of represented institutional entities and directed relationships. Absence from the topology does not establish absence in the real institution.

## Observatory Assessment

A deterministic description of the retrieved evidence landscape, including composition and concentration. It describes the evidence set; it does not determine fitness for a decision.

## Decision Readiness

Domain-level deterministic evaluation used by Evidence Fitness. It records scores, grades, supporting sources, keyword breadth, limitations, and missing requirements.

## Evidence Fitness

Question-aware assessment of whether available evidence is suitable for a classified decision. It evaluates domain coverage, authority, evidence role, scope, directness, breadth, and document-family independence.

## Decision Brief

An executive knowledge product combining grounded synthesis with deterministic readiness, Evidence Fitness, topology, workforce, evidence-map, and participation panels. It supports—but does not replace—human judgment.

## Academic Workforce Planning

The implemented decision type for institution-wide or unit-level academic staffing questions. Its eight canonical evidence domains are Instructional Demand, Faculty Capacity, Service Teaching Dependence, Accreditation and External Constraints, Enrollment Trends, Financial Implications, Strategic Priority Alignment, and One-Line Loss Scenario.

## Liberal Learning Core (LLC)

CNU’s canonical term for institution-wide core requirements and Areas of Inquiry. “General Education” may appear in legacy source data but is not the preferred presentation terminology.

## Scenario Modeling

The planned fifth layer for comparing explicit alternatives and consequences. No production workforce scenario engine is currently implemented.

## Institutional Digital Twin

The aspirational sixth layer: a temporal, evidence-backed representation of institutional entities, capabilities, relationships, constraints, and change. The current topology is not a complete digital twin.

## Provenance

Metadata describing origin, acquisition method, source boundary, timestamps, parser, path, and other information needed to audit an observation.
