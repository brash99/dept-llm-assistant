# Observations

## Successes

- LLM consistently distinguished institutional evidence from external comparator evidence.
- Purdue ABET report was treated as an example rather than evidence about CNU.
- Budget proposal correctly identified as a planning document rather than an approved institutional fact.
- Decision Brief naturally discussed uncertainty.

## Remaining Weaknesses

- External Standard classification still needs refinement.
- Prompt occasionally overstates general ABET guidance.
- Evidence hierarchy is implicit rather than explicitly referenced throughout the narrative.

## Architectural Insight

Evidence Classes appear to improve reasoning quality without modifying retrieval.

This suggests that semantic organization after retrieval may be as important as retrieval quality itself.
