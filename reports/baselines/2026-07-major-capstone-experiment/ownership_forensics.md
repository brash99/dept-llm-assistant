# Undergraduate Major Ownership Forensics

## Scope and method

This report compares Quentin Kidd's administrative department-major mapping with the institutional structure explicitly published in the **2025–26 Christopher Newport University Undergraduate Catalog**:

`data/acquisition/catalogs/2025-26-undergraduate_catalog.pdf`

Page citations below use the printed catalog page number, which matches the PDF page number for the cited material. “Catalog owner” means the unit under which the catalog places, administers, or describes the curriculum. It does not necessarily mean the unit used for degree-completion reporting, budgeting, or administrative data aggregation.

No disagreement is resolved here. Where catalog evidence is stronger for curricular ownership, that conclusion is stated without changing the governed registry.

## Detailed findings

### Neuroscience

**Administrative owner:** Quentin's table contains two Neuroscience rows: one assigned to `IDST` and another assigned to `PSYC`.

**Catalog owner:** The catalog presents a standalone **Neuroscience Program** housed within the **College of Natural and Behavioral Sciences**. It does not place the major directly under Psychology, Biology, or Interdisciplinary Studies.

**Catalog citations:**

- **Page 42, Degree Programs:** lists “Neuroscience” as a Bachelor of Science program.
- **Page 185, Neuroscience / Neuroscience Program:** identifies “Dr. Andrew J. Velkey, Director” and states: “The neuroscience program is an interdisciplinary degree program housed within the College of Natural and Behavioral Sciences.”
- **Page 185:** states that successful completion results in a “Bachelor of Science in Neuroscience.”
- **Pages 185–186:** describes an interdisciplinary curriculum and faculty spanning neuroscience and ancillary disciplines.

**Structural determination:**

- Department heading: none.
- School heading: none.
- College heading: College of Natural and Behavioral Sciences, stated in program prose.
- Directly under a department: no.
- Interdisciplinary program: yes.
- Curriculum versus administration: the catalog establishes program-level curricular administration, not departmental ownership by Psychology or IDST.

**Classification:** `interdisciplinary_governance` and `unresolved_conflict`.

**Evidence judgment:** The catalog is stronger evidence for current **curricular governance** because it explicitly names the program, director, degree, and college home. Quentin's dual IDST/PSYC rows may reflect historical graduates, reporting attribution, or departmental participation, but they do not establish current catalog ownership.

### American Studies

**Administrative owner:** `IDST`.

**Catalog owner:** **Department of Leadership and American Studies**, within the College of Social Sciences. The conferred degree is a **Bachelor of Arts in Interdisciplinary Studies with a major in American Studies**.

**Catalog citations:**

- **Page 42, Degree Programs:** lists “American Studies major” under Bachelor of Arts, Interdisciplinary Studies.
- **Page 62, College and School Designations:** places Leadership and American Studies in the College of Social Sciences.
- **Page 145, Leadership and American Studies:** explicitly identifies the “Department of Leadership and American Studies.”
- **Page 145:** states that the department offers “a Bachelor of Arts degree in Interdisciplinary Studies with majors and minors in American studies and leadership studies.”
- **Page 145, Center for American Studies:** states that the Center for American Studies is “in the Department of Leadership and American Studies.”
- **Page 145, American Studies Major:** identifies a Director of American Studies and begins the governed curriculum.
- **Pages 145–149:** contain the American Studies curriculum and concentrations.

**Structural determination:**

- Department heading: Department of Leadership and American Studies.
- School heading: none.
- College heading: College of Social Sciences.
- Directly under a department: yes.
- Interdisciplinary program: interdisciplinary curriculum administered inside a department.
- Curriculum versus administration: `IDST` describes the degree shell; LAMS is the catalog's departmental administrative and curricular home.

**Classification:** `unresolved_conflict` with a `reporting_vs_curricular_ownership` distinction.

**Evidence judgment:** The catalog is stronger evidence for current curricular and administrative ownership. Quentin's `IDST` mapping is understandable as degree-reporting ownership because the diploma program is Interdisciplinary Studies, but the catalog directly places the major, center, director, faculty, and curriculum in LAMS.

### Studio Art

**Administrative owner:** `MTD`.

**Catalog owner:** **Department of Fine Art and Art History**, within the **School of the Arts**, College of Arts and Humanities.

**Catalog citations:**

- **Page 62, College and School Designations:** places Fine Art and Art History in the School of the Arts within the College of Arts and Humanities.
- **Page 231, School of the Arts:** states that the School brings together the Department of Fine Art and Art History and the Department of Music, Theatre, and Dance.
- **Page 232, Fine Art and Art History:** explicitly identifies the “Department of Fine Art and Art History.”
- **Page 232, Bachelor of Arts Degree in Art and Art History:** lists “Studio Art major” under that department, separately from the Art History major.
- **Page 233:** refers to the departmental senior-seminar requirement for students “in the studio art or art history major.”

**Structural determination:**

- Department heading: Department of Fine Art and Art History.
- School heading: School of the Arts.
- College heading: College of Arts and Humanities.
- Directly under a department: yes.
- Interdisciplinary program: no.
- Curriculum versus administration: both are assigned to Fine Art and Art History by the catalog.

**Classification:** `unresolved_conflict`.

**Evidence judgment:** The catalog clearly provides stronger current evidence than the administrative mapping. It names the department and places the full Studio Art curriculum there. The `MTD` assignment may reflect reporting practice or a workbook classification issue, but it is not supported as curricular ownership by the current catalog.

### Health Studies / Health Sciences

**Administrative owner:** No corresponding row appears in Quentin's supplied table.

**Catalog owner:** A standalone, director-led **Health Studies** interdisciplinary curriculum leading to a **Bachelor of Arts in Interdisciplinary Studies**. The catalog does not place it under a conventional department or school.

**Catalog citations:**

- **Page 42, Degree Programs:** lists “Health Sciences major” under Bachelor of Arts, Interdisciplinary Studies.
- **Page 121, Health Studies:** provides a standalone heading, names Dr. Alice Veksler as Director, and labels the program “Bachelor of Arts Degree in Interdisciplinary Studies — Major in Health Studies.”
- **Page 121:** describes “The Curriculum in Health Studies” and says the program draws from all three colleges.
- **Page 121:** also uses the phrase “major in health sciences,” creating a current-catalog naming inconsistency.

**Structural determination:**

- Department heading: none.
- School heading: none.
- Academic-unit heading: Health Studies.
- Directly under a department: no.
- Interdisciplinary program: yes.
- Curriculum versus administration: administered as a director-led interdisciplinary program; no department owner is stated.

**Classification:** `naming_difference` and `interdisciplinary_governance`.

**Evidence judgment:** “Health Studies” and “Health Sciences” are used inconsistently inside the same catalog. The evidence supports one interdisciplinary curriculum, but it does not justify assigning a conventional department owner.

### Interdisciplinary Studies

- **Page 140:** states that a proposed program must not be achievable through a regular departmental major and includes the IDST 490 capstone.

**Structural determination:**

- Department heading: none.
- School heading: none.
- Academic-unit heading: Interdisciplinary Studies.
- Directly under a department: no.
- Interdisciplinary program: yes.
- Curriculum versus administration: the program itself coordinates the individualized curriculum.

**Classification:** `agreement` at the program/reporting level and `interdisciplinary_governance`.

## Conclusions

1. **Art History is an ownership agreement.**
2. **Studio Art is a direct ownership conflict.** The catalog clearly places it in Fine Art and Art History, not Music, Theatre, and Dance.
3. **American Studies exposes degree-reporting ownership versus curricular ownership.** `IDST` is the degree shell, while LAMS is the current catalog department administering the major.
4. **Neuroscience is independently governed as an interdisciplinary program in the College of Natural and Behavioral Sciences.** The catalog does not support collapsing it into PSYC or IDST.
5. **Health Studies/Health Sciences is both a naming inconsistency and an interdisciplinary program.** The current catalog does not identify a conventional department owner.
6. **Interdisciplinary Studies is a legitimate program-level owner/coordinator, not a department.**
7. The four historical names are absent as current standalone majors. Their appearance in administrative degree data should be preserved as historical/reporting evidence until older catalogs establish effective dates and successor relationships.

No governed ownership decision has been changed by this investigation.
